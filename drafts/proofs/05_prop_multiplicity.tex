\begin{proof}[Proof of Proposition~\ref{prop:multiplicity}]
The proposition establishes that different attribution functions sustain different equilibria in the same material game. Sufficient conditions appear in Online Appendix OA.2.4.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step 1: Attribution determines beliefs.}
By ABE4, attributed beliefs $\tilde{h}_i^{(2,j)}[\phi] = \phi_i(\theta_j, x_j, \omega_i)$ and $\tilde{h}_i^{(2,j)}[\phi'] = \phi'_i(\theta_j, x_j, \omega_i)$ differ when $\phi \neq \phi'$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step 2: Beliefs affect payoffs.}
When $\partial \psi_i / \partial \tilde{h}_i^{(2,j)} \neq 0$, different attributed beliefs induce different psychological payoffs.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step 3: Payoffs shift equilibria.}
By Theorem~\ref{thm:existence}, ABE exists for both $\Gamma(\phi)$ and $\Gamma(\phi')$. When best-response separation holds, $s^*[\phi] \neq s^*[\phi']$.
\end{proof}
